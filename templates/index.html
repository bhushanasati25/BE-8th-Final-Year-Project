Project Description



The goal of this project is to analyze the sentiment of social media data related to top colleges in the World. The project will involve collecting social media data from various platforms such as Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram, and using natural language processing (NLP) techniques to analyze the sentiment of the posts. The project will focus on analyzing the sentiment of posts related to the top colleges in the World, which will be determined based on rankings from publications such as News & World Report. The sentiment analysis will help identify the overall sentiment of the posts related to these colleges, and also help identify any specific issues or topics that are driving positive or negative sentiment. The automatic classification of text into positive, negative, or neutral sentiment using the technique of sentiment analysis has grown in popularity as a tool for studying social media data. In this study, we use information from social media sites to do a sentiment analysis of the top institutions in the United States. We want to find out how people feel about these institutions generally and determine any potential influencing variables.The world of social media is altering as a result of Web 2.0's introduction. Not only are people using social media to connect, exchange information, and express their personal opinions with others, but businesses can also utilize it to connect, understand, and enhance their goods and services.

FAQ



Sentiment analysis is the process of using natural language processing (NLP) techniques to extract subjective information from text, such as opinions, emotions, and attitudes.

Sentiment analysis has many applications, such as social media monitoring, customer feedback analysis, brand reputation management, market research, and political polling.

Sentiment analysis typically involves preprocessing text data to remove noise, such as stop words and punctuation, and then using machine learning algorithms or rule-based systems to classify the sentiment of the text as positive, negative, or neutral.

Some challenges in sentiment analysis include identifying sarcasm and irony, dealing with ambiguous and context-dependent language, and handling multilingual data.

Some popular tools and libraries for sentiment analysis include NLTK, TextBlob, spaCy, VADER, and IBM Watson Natural Language Understanding.
